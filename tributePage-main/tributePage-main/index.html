Atal Bihari Vajpayee

A Statesman and Poet of India

Atal Bihari Vajpayee (December 25, 1924 - August 16, 2018) was an Indian statesman who served three terms as the Prime Minister of India. Known for his eloquence, Vajpayee was also a noted poet and a senior leader of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP). His tenure was marked by efforts to improve relations with Pakistan, economic reforms, and nuclear tests that established India as a nuclear power. Vajpayee's leadership and vision continue to inspire many.

Atal Bihari Vajpayee's political career spanned over five decades, during which he was instrumental in shaping modern India. His speeches and writings reflect his deep commitment to the country's progress and his belief in democratic ideals.

Here's a timeline of Atal Bihari Vajpayee's life:

  • 1924: Born on December 25 in Gwalior, India.
  • 1942: Participates in the Quit India Movement.
  • 1957: Elected to the Lok Sabha for the first time.
  • 1996: Serves his first term as Prime Minister of India.
  • 1998-2004: Serves his second and third terms as Prime Minister of India.
  • 2018: Passes away on August 16 in New Delhi, India.
